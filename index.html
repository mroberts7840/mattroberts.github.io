The Offside Rule Explained

A player is offside if, when the ball is played, they are closer to the opposition goal line than both the ball and the last defender.

However, a player can be in an offside position provided they are not interfering with play. Interfering with play is defined by FIFA as playing or touching the ball passed or touched by a team-mate.

Other definitions of interfering with play, other than playing or touching the ball, are

  • Interfering with an opponent

  • Gaining an advantage

You can't be offside if :

You receive the ball directly from a goal kick, throw-in or corner

You are in your own half of the pitch
